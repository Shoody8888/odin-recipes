Kopytka or dumplings made of boiled and mashed potatoes, flour and eggs.

  • 1 kg of potatoes
  • 200 grams of flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon of salt

  1. Peel the potatoes and boil them in salted water. Drain and immediately mash with a press to a perfectly smooth puree. Transfer to a bowl and cool completely.
  2. Add flour, egg and a pinch of salt. Combine the ingredients into a uniform dough.
  3. Put it on a table sprinkled with flour and divide it into 6 parts. Roll each piece into a thumb-thick roll by rolling it in flour.
  4. Slightly flatten the roller and cut with a knife into pieces about 1.5 - 2 cm wide.
  5. Cook in batches: place in boiling salted water in a large pot and cook for about 3 minutes from the time they float to the surface or until tender.
  6. During cooking, gently stir the water with a wooden spoon, checking if the dumplings did not stick to the bottom. Remove with a slotted spoon and place on plates.
